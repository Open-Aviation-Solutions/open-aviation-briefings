# Stalling — In-flight Notes

_CASA Recreational Pilot License (Aeroplane) — Lesson 5_

> Say **"ease forward"** not "push down." Say **"reduce angle of attack"** — reinforce the concept, not just the action.

---

# Before starting the plane

- Student reads checks; work through together
- Check seat locked; note nose attitude with fingers on the horizon for level flight
- Point out ASI white arc — flap operating range
- Brief engine start malfunctions: flooded start procedure, what to do with a live magneto on shutdown
- Confirm HASELL will be called before every stalling exercise

---

# During startup

- Student reads checks and manages start; instructor supervises
- Note any carburettor heat check — engine should respond (slight RPM drop then recovery)

---

# After departing / established in the climb

- Climb to TODO: ft AGL and transit to training area
- Brief slow flight coming up: "We'll slow the aircraft down to approach speed and just above the stall — you'll feel the controls become mushy. That's normal."

---

# Slow flight — approach speed (flaps retracted)

HASELL complete. Configure for slow flight.

- DDM: Set power TODO: RPM, raise nose, trim
- Direct: "Tell me what you notice about the controls"
- Monitor: Maintain coordinated flight; assist with trim if needed
- Transition back to cruise: power up, lower nose, retract flaps in stages

---

# Slow flight — approach configuration (flaps extended)

HASELL complete. Configure for approach configuration.

- DDM: Flap as for approach, set power for level flight at Vapp
- Direct: "Notice the lower nose attitude compared to flaps-retracted slow flight"
- Direct: "Add a little more back pressure — what do you hear?"
- Monitor: Stall warning activation; maintain coordination

---

# Stall from straight and level — power off, recovery without power

HASELL complete. Clear for power-off stall.

- DDM: Reduce power to idle; raise nose steadily; maintain coordination with rudder
- Direct: "Tell me what you feel and what you see"
- Monitor: Full stall entry — nose drops, possible buffet
- On stall: "Recover!" (or student self-initiates)
- Recovery: ease forward → level wings with rudder → establish glide → note height loss
- Follow-through: discuss what they felt, reinforce AOA concept

---

# Stall from straight and level — recovery with full power

HASELL complete. Demonstrate first, then student flies.

- DDM: Raise nose to stall entry; at stall — forward, full power, rudder, level up, climb
- Direct: "Notice the tendency to yaw with power — hold it with rudder"
- Monitor: Check for secondary stall tendency on recovery; secondary stall comes from pulling back too hard before the wing is flying
- Repeat: student flies two or more with full recovery each time

---

# Stall from approach configuration (landing config stall)

HASELL complete. Flap extended to approach setting.

- DDM: Slow to Vapp with flap; raise nose to stall entry
- Direct: "Notice the lower nose attitude at the stall — this is what an inadvertent approach stall looks like"
- Monitor: Recovery — forward, power, level wings; retract flap in stages as speed increases
- Debrief point: relate this to the danger of flying too slow on final approach

---

# Wing drop / spin avoidance — instructor demonstration only

HASELL complete. Student follows through; instructor retains control throughout.

- Enter stall with slight yaw (slight rudder) to provoke wing drop
- Demonstrate: at wing drop — forward AND rudder into the raised wing (not aileron); recovery
- Direct: "Notice: aileron into a dropping wing at the stall can worsen the roll. Rudder is the correct input."
- Discuss: spin entry — yaw + stall = autorotation; how HASELL and recovery technique prevent this

---

# Return to aerodrome

- Set cruise power; CTAF call and join circuit
- Student flies where able; instructor demonstrates as required
- Note fuel state; confirm all checks complete
