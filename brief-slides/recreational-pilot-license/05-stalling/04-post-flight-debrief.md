# Flight review — slow flight and stall recognition

_Stalling_

**See if you can describe each of the following:**

**Slow flight**
- What did the controls feel like as we slowed down toward approach speed?
- What happened just before the stall warning activated?

**Stall recognition**
- What were the first signs that the stall was coming?
- At what point did you (or would you) have acted?

<!-- If the student struggled to identify the buffet or the mushy controls as stall cues, reinforce those as the primary physical cues — ahead of the horn. The horn is a backup, not the primary warning. -->

---

# Flight review — stall recovery

_Stalling_

**Stall recovery — what happened in the air:**

- What was the first thing you did (or should have done) when the stall occurred?
- Was there any tendency for the nose to yaw? What did you do about it?
- Did we experience a secondary stall? If so, what caused it?

**Landing configuration stall**
- How was the nose attitude different with flap extended?
- What does that tell us about the danger of an inadvertent approach stall?

<!-- If a secondary stall occurred, address it here without making it a failure — it is a learning moment. The cause is almost always pulling back too quickly after the forward input. -->

---

# Flight review — HASELL and procedures

_Stalling_

**Checking our processes:**

- Can you run through HASELL from memory?
- Did the clearing turn feel thorough? Did we ever rush it?
- What was the purpose of the carburettor heat during HASELL?

<!-- HASELL from memory: Height, Airframe, Security, Engine, Location, Lookout. If the student cannot recall it yet, practise it now — it should be automatic before solo. -->

# Training outcomes — slow speed and stalling

_Stalling_

All criteria today are at **Level 2 — Developing**: safe to practise under direct supervision.

| MOS Reference | Area | Criteria |
|---------------|------|---------|
| A3.5 | Slow speeds | Pre-manoeuvre checks; stable flight at minimum approach speeds; respond to stall warnings |
| A5.1 | Stall entry and recovery | Recognise stall symptoms; recover without and with power; recover from approach config stall |
| A5.1(d) | Recovery steps | Reduce AOA; prevent yaw; use power/height; avoid secondary stall; re-establish flight path |

---

# Training outcomes — spin avoidance and unusual attitudes

_Stalling_

These criteria are at **Level 3 — Introduced**: training has been received; consistency not yet expected.

| A5.2 | Spin avoidance (demo) | HASELL; recognise wing drop; correct recovery with rudder (not aileron) |
