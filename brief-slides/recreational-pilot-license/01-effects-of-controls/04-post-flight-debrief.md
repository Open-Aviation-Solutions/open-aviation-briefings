# How did it go?

**Let's start with your experience:**

- What felt natural? What felt unexpected?
- Did anything surprise you about how the aeroplane responded?
- What's the one thing that stuck with you most from today?

<!-- Let the student lead. Don't jump straight to corrections — build confidence first, then address gaps. A positive start makes the rest of the debrief more productive. -->

---

# Controls review — elevator and ailerons

**See if you can describe each of the following effects:**

**Elevator**

- What happened when you applied back pressure (pulled it towards you) to the control column?
- What else changed as you held the control column backward or forward?

**Ailerons**

- What happened when you turned the control column to the left or the right?
- What else did you notice?

<!-- If the student can articulate the secondary effects unprompted, that's the real test of understanding. Prompt with "and what else?" before giving the answer yourself. Use the 3D model again to help visualise. -->

---

# Controls review — rudder and ancillary controls

**Rudder**

- What happened when you applied left or right rudder?
- What followed?

**Trim**

- What did trim do to your workload? Or what did you notice as the trim was changed?

**Throttle and flaps**

- What happened to airspeed with full power?
- What happened when you extended flap?

Any other thoughts about the flight that you want to point out?

<!-- If the student struggles with secondary effects, revisit them briefly rather than moving on. Secondary effects are the most common weak point at this stage. -->

# Training outcomes — ground operations

All criteria today are at **Level 3 — Introduced**: training has been received. Consistency is not yet expected.

| Area | Criteria covered |
|------|-----------------|
| Pre-flight documentation | Completed pre-flight administration |
| Pre-flight inspection | Internal and external check; locking devices removed and stowed |
| Engine start and stop | Start and after-start; shutdown and after-shutdown; ground surface and propeller care |
| Taxiing | Markings and right-of-way; safe speed; spacing; centre of taxiway; hazard avoidance; engine management |
| Post-flight | Shutdown; post-flight inspection and securing; post-flight documentation |

<!-- Run through these as a conversational tick-off, not a lecture. If something notable happened during ground operations — good or otherwise — mention it specifically here. -->

---

# Training outcomes — lookout and air work

**Visual lookout** (Level 3 — Introduced)

- Did we maintain a systematic scan before each manoeuvre?
- Clock code for calling traffic — comfortable with it?

**In the air**

- Control technique: smooth inputs, or any tendency to snatch?
- Handover: was "I have control / you have control" always clear?
- Anything to carry forward to the next lesson?

**Remember:** Learning and understanding the **effects of the aeroplane controls** is one of the most fundamental units of understanding that will help us fly safely throughout our pilot journey!

# Next steps

**Lesson 2 — Straight and Level Flight**

- We'll be applying what you learned today: using elevator and throttle to hold a constant altitude and heading
- Trim becomes our best friend
- We'll also be continuing to take more responsibility for the ground work, taxiing and take-off.

**Before next lesson**

- Review the primary and secondary effects of each control (see the NZ handout that you can take)
- **Challenge 1:** See if you can work out how the trim is physically doing what you felt in the plane, so that you can explain it to me next lesson.
- **Challenge 2:** Explain why (or how) the trim reduces your workload in flight.

**Right now**

- Sign off today's training record
- Any questions?
