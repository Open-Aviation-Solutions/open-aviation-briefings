# Effects of Controls — In-flight Lesson

_CASA Recreational Pilot License (Aeroplane) — Lesson 1_

> Say **"pitch the nose up"** — not "pull back on the control column." The student should think in terms of what the aeroplane is doing outside, not what their hand is doing.

---

# Before starting the plane

- Ensure seat is locked and learner is comfortable
- Check the attitude — the angle the nose of the plane makes with the horizon and have the learner check how many fingers this is for them (basically for level flying).
- Point out the white arc on the ASI and have learner check flaps extension, noting the click feel. Note that we always change the flaps in stages since it affects our lift — we can sink when retracting flaps due to less lift (at the same speed).
- Go through "My aircraft", "Your aircraft"

---

# During startup

- Student to read checks and we work through them together.
- Check if student would like to control the elevator and throttle during take-off? (Fine either way, no pressure).

---

# After departing and established in the climb

On the way to the training area.

---

# Elevator — primary and secondary

- DDM: Use the elevator to gently pitch down and then up a few times
- DDM: Note airspeed then pitch forward to level flight using fingers and hold at that attitude, watch speed. Slowly let nose rise back towards horizon and watch the speed.

---

# Ailerons — primary and secondary

At the training area — trim for level flight with 2300 RPM, then:

- DDM: Use the ailerons to gently roll the plane left and right around the longitudinal axis.
- DDM: Roll the plane to a 30° angle of bank and watch for the slip and yaw.

---

# Rudder — primary and secondary

Re-establish straight and level, then:

- DDM: Use the rudder to gently yaw the plane left and right around the normal plane.
- DDM: Gently but firmly push the rudder all the way to the floor and observe what happens.

---

# Trim — effect of trim

Re-establish straight and level, then:

- Ask student to take the control and keep us level (check fingers). Explain what I'll be doing with the trim (going to both extremes before returning) and what they will feel.
- Note trim position for return.

---

# Throttle

Re-establish straight and level with 2300 and start heading back towards the airport, CTAF call etc., then:

- Ask learner to report the current speed (should be around 90–100 kt).
- E-P: Slowly move the throttle to full throttle and observe what happens.
- Ask learner to confirm speed.
- E-P: Now slowly move the throttle back to 2300 RPM and observe what happens.
- Ask learner to report the current speed.
- E-P: Now slowly move the throttle back to 2100 RPM and observe what happens.
- Ask learner to report the current speed.

---

# Flaps

Leave RPM at 2100 and, when reaching 4000, trim for straight and level.

- Ask learner to report speed and verify that we're in the white range.
- E-P: Extend flaps to first notch and note what happens (possibly to second if needed).
- Re-trim to ensure straight and level, with speed around 80 kt.
- E-P: Retract flaps (in stages if necessary) and note what happens.

---

# Stability

If there's time, DDM stability by small rudder and roll, then let go of controls.

My aircraft, then join circuit and land, demonstrating as we go.
