# Language notes

- **"climb attitude"** / **"descent attitude"** — nose position relative to the horizon, not "pull back" or "push forward"
- **"level off"** — not "stop climbing"
- **VY** = "best rate" (most feet per minute); **VX** = "best angle" (most feet per distance)
- **"power idle"** — not "engine off"; the engine is still running at idle

---

# Before starting the plane

- Seat locked, student comfortable
- Review altimeter subscale — set to QNH
- Confirm student remembers climb and glide attitudes from theory
- Review "My aircraft / Your aircraft" handover phrases

---

# During startup

- Student reads checks; work through together
- Brief on engine temperature monitoring during prolonged climb

---

# Climb to training area

- Point out climb attitude relative to horizon; note VY vs cruise climb appearance
- Check engine temperatures on climb — introduce habit of monitoring during prolonged climbs

---

# In the training area

Establish straight and level at cruise power before beginning exercises.

For each exercise: **Demonstrate** → **Direct** → **Monitor**.

# Normal (cruise) climb entry and level off

- **Demonstrate**: apply climb power → pitch to cruise climb attitude (87 KIAS) → trim; then anticipate level-off → lower nose → reduce power → trim
- **Direct**: student initiates climb from level, holds attitude, levels off at nominated altitude
- **Monitor**: student manages full cycle — climb entry, attitude hold, level off — unaided

---

# Best rate of climb (VY)

- **Demonstrate**: full power → pitch to VY attitude (75 KIAS) → note reduced forward visibility and slower acceleration; level off
- **Direct**: student establishes VY climb, holds attitude, levels off
- **Monitor**: student transitions to VY and back to level unaided

---

# Best angle of climb (VX)

- **Demonstrate**: full power → pitch to VX attitude (63 KIAS) → note steep attitude and slow speed; brief only — level off promptly
- **Direct**: student establishes VX for a few seconds, notes differences from VY
- **Monitor**: student can identify and hold the VX attitude; prompt recovery to level

---

# Powered descent and level off

- **Demonstrate**: reduce power (2200) → hold level attitude briefly → lower nose to cruise descent attitude (90 KIAS) → trim; level off anticipating altitude
- **Direct**: student initiates and holds powered descent, levels off at nominated altitude
- **Monitor**: student manages full cycle unaided

---

# Glide (power off)

- **Demonstrate**: reduce power to idle → pitch to best glide attitude (73 KIAS) → trim; note silence and descent rate
- **Direct**: student establishes glide, holds best glide attitude
- **Monitor**: student establishes and holds glide unaided; apply power before getting too low

---

# Approach descent (flap stages)

- **Demonstrate**: reduce power (1700 RPM, carb heat) → extend flap one stage → note pitch change → re-trim; add second stage; note further pitch change. 70 KIAS
- **Direct**: student applies flap in stages, adjusts attitude each time, trims
- **Monitor**: student manages flap extension and re-trim independently; then retract in stages

---

# Return to airport

- Retract flaps in stages if extended; establish normal cruise
- CTAF call as appropriate
- Let student fly straight and level during return for consolidation
- Introduce turning to a heading for a taster of next week — could point out the rate-1 turn markers on the turn coordinator

# Configuration reference

| Configuration | Power (approx) | Speed (approx) | Notes |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|-------|
| Cruise climb | Full | 87 KIAS | Normal transit climb |
| Best rate (VY) | Full | 75 KIAS | Most ft/min |
| Best angle (VX) | Full | 63 KIAS | Most ft per distance |
| Best glide | Idle | 73 KIAS | Power off |
| Cruise descent | 2200 RPM | 90 KIAS (?) | Gentle powered descent |
