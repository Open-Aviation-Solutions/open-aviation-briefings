CASA Recreational Pilot License (Aeroplane) — CASA Sample Syllabus Lesson 4.

This lesson is on turning — something the student has already been practising, but here we
learn the *why* as well as how to control a turn during climb and descent. The theory brief
covers the forces acting on the aeroplane in a banked turn, coordinated (balanced) turns and
adverse yaw, the entry/maintenance/exit of medium level, climbing and descending turns, the
overbank and underbank tendencies, and recognition and recovery from a spiral dive. In the
flight we practise medium level turns, climbing turns and descending turns, and observe an
instructor demonstration of a spiral dive recovery.

The pre-flight brief (~15 min, immediately before the flight) works best as an
interactive **whiteboard session**, not slides — stepping through the flight plan, threats,
and the spiral-dive recovery rehearsal together with the student. These printable notes give
you the briefing components (aim, flight walk-through, threats, lookout / balance / spiral
recovery) with prompts and fill-in blanks, plus a front page to sketch your own whiteboard
plan and running order. Although it will be helpful to plan and step through it yourself
before the lesson, remember: **modelling the process** of the pre-flight brief is more
important than presenting a polished pre-flight brief. This is why we do it interactively
rather than a presentation.

## Useful resources for students

## Example email to students before the lesson

If you are an instructor, feel free to modify this for your own use. Sending an email a few
days before a lesson is a great way to engage people in the learning before they even arrive
(if they have time and capacity).

> **Subject:** Lesson 4 — Turning
>
> Hi [name],
>
> Our next lesson is on **turning** — something you've already been practising in your
> flights so far, but that we'll learn more of the *why* as well as how to control a turn
> during climb and descent. We'll spend the long briefing understanding why an aeroplane
> turns the way it does (it's more interesting than it sounds!), and then in the flight we'll
> put it into practice: medium level turns, climbing turns, and descending turns.
>
> We'll also do a brief instructor demonstration of a **spiral dive** — a situation every
> pilot needs to be able to recognise and recover from safely. Don't worry — I'll be at the
> controls for that one.
>
> **To prepare:**
>
> - Watch this short video animation on how the lift force changes during a turn — it covers
>   the same physics we'll discuss in the briefing: [How Planes Make Turns](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7tWOyOu-QG4) (~5 min)
> - Think about what you already know about the "ball" (inclinometer) in the cockpit — we'll
>   talk about keeping it centred during turns.
>
> **When:** [day]
>
> See you then!
