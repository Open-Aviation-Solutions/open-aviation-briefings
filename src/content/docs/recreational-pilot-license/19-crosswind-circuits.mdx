CASA Recreational Pilot License (Aeroplane) — CASA Sample Syllabus Lesson 19.

This lesson introduces crosswind circuit technique, building on the student's established circuit skills from Lessons 6–15. The lesson covers aircraft and personal crosswind limits, how to calculate or look up the crosswind component, and the two main drift-correction techniques — the crab method and the wing-low (sideslip) method — applied from take-off through to the landing roll. The student also reviews engine failure after take-off and in the circuit area, and practises radio communications in non-standard circuit situations.

## Theory Brief

Crosswind limits (aircraft, operator, and personal), the crosswind component calculation, and the two main techniques for correcting drift: the crab method and the wing-low method. The theory covers each phase of the circuit — take-off roll, lift-off, approach, touchdown, and landing roll — and establishes the go-around decision as the primary airmanship tool. The pre-flight brief (~15 min, immediately before the flight) works best as an interactive **whiteboard session**, not slides — stepping through the circuit, the crosswind corrections, and the threats together with the student. These printable notes give you the briefing components (aim, today's flight walk-through, threats, directional-control airmanship emphasis) with prompts and fill-in blanks, plus a front page to sketch your own whiteboard plan and running order. Plan and run through it yourself before the lesson, but remember: **modelling the process** of the pre-flight brief is more important than presenting a polished one. This is why we do it interactively rather than as a presentation.

## Example email to students before the lesson

If you are an instructor, feel free to modify this for your own use. Sending an email a few days before a lesson is a great way to engage people in the learning before they even arrive (if they have time and capacity).

> **Subject:** Lesson 19 — Crosswind Circuits
>
> Hi [name],
>
> This next lesson builds directly on all the circuit practice you've done — the difference is that today the wind won't be conveniently aligned with the runway. We'll be flying crosswind circuits, which means learning to correct for the drift that a crosswind creates, especially at touchdown and during the landing roll.
>
> Before the flight we'll have a short theory session (~30 min) covering crosswind limits, how to calculate the crosswind component, and the two main techniques for correcting drift on approach. Then we'll fly for about an hour of circuit practice.
>
> If you'd like to prepare, the NZ CAA has a clear one-page [Crosswind circuit whiteboard](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/assets/licensing-and-certification/flight-instructor-guide/whiteboards/fig-whiteboard-ct-cwc.pdf) [1] worth a look. You can also work through the full briefings on this site [2].
>
> **When:** [day]
>
> See you then!
