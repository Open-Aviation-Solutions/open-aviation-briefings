CASA Recreational Pilot License (Aeroplane) — CASA Sample Syllabus Lesson 3.

This lesson covers climbing and descending — the fundamental techniques for changing
altitude safely and efficiently. Building directly on straight and level flight, we apply
the same **Power + Attitude = Performance** formula to climb and descent manoeuvres: the
aerodynamic forces at work, the entry and level-off sequences, the three types of climb
(cruise, best rate and best angle) and the three types of descent (cruise, glide and
approach with flap), and what the instruments are telling you throughout.

The pre-flight brief (~15 min, immediately before the flight) works best as an
interactive **whiteboard session**, not slides — stepping through the flight plan, the
TAF, threats and airmanship together with the student. These printable notes give you the
briefing components (aim, flight walk-through, threats, lookout and level-off) with prompts
and fill-in blanks, plus a front page to sketch your own whiteboard plan and running order.
Although it will be helpful to plan and step through it yourself before the lesson,
remember: **modelling the process** of the pre-flight brief is more important than
presenting a polished pre-flight brief. This is why we do it interactively rather than a
presentation.

## Example email to students before the lesson

If you are an instructor, feel free to modify this for your own use. Sending an email a few
days before a lesson is a great way to engage people in the learning before they even arrive
(if they have time and capacity).

> **Subject:** Climbing and Descending lesson this [day]
>
> Good morning/afternoon [name]!
>
> Our next lesson together covers **climbing and descending** — the fundamental techniques
> for changing altitude safely and efficiently. Building directly on what we covered in
> straight and level flight, we'll apply the same Power + Attitude = Performance formula to
> climb and descent manoeuvres.
>
> During our theory session we'll explore the aerodynamic forces at work during climbs and
> descents, the entry and level-off sequences, and the three types of climb you'll use
> throughout your training: normal cruise climb, best rate of climb, and best angle of climb.
> We'll also cover gliding and powered descents, and what the instruments are telling you
> during these manoeuvres.
>
> In the air we'll practise transitioning between straight and level, climbing, and
> descending flight — and you'll experience firsthand how the aeroplane behaves differently
> at each climb speed.
>
> See you [day],
>
> — Michael
